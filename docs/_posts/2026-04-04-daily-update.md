The post-Cursor-3.0 shakeout is reshaping the leaderboard. For the first time in our tracking period, Cursor and GitHub Copilot are tied at 90 — a result that would have seemed unlikely two weeks ago when Copilot held a comfortable four-point lead. Google's Gemma 4 open-source release adds another dimension to the week, giving developers a capable local alternative to cloud-hosted models.

## Cursor: Holding at 90

Cursor's tie with Copilot at 90 reflects sustained post-launch momentum from the 3.0 release. The parallel agents feature is generating significant developer interest, particularly among teams that manage multiple repositories. Pro pricing dropped from $20 to $16 per month on the annual plan — a cut that removes a common objection for individual developers weighing the switch from Copilot.

## GitHub Copilot: SDK and Pricing Response

GitHub responded to Cursor's pricing cut with its own: Copilot Pro dropped from $10/month to $8.33/month ($100/year). The Copilot SDK public preview, now available in five languages, is the more strategically significant move — it lets enterprise teams build custom agents on Copilot's runtime rather than waiting for GitHub's roadmap. Org-level custom instructions also graduated to general availability.

## Google: Gemma 4 Open Release

Google released Gemma 4 in four sizes — 2B, 7B, 14B, and 31B — under the Apache 2.0 license. The 31B model ranked third on the Arena AI open model leaderboard, a result that puts it ahead of every open model except the top two. For developers who need local inference without cloud dependency, this is the most capable open option released this year.

## Claude Code: Leak Aftermath

The Claude Code source leak fallout continues. The exposed KAIROS daemon mode — a persistent background assistant — is now the subject of detailed community analysis. Some developers are building experimental implementations based on the leaked feature flags, a situation Anthropic's legal team is monitoring closely. Claude Code's score held at 91 despite the controversy, suggesting the technical community's response has been more curious than alarmed.

## Copilot Privacy Deadline

The April 24 deadline for GitHub Copilot data training opt-out is three weeks away. With Copilot already under scrutiny from the PR ad bug, the deadline is a second front of negative sentiment. Community discussion on Reddit and Hacker News is trending toward the view that the opt-out should have been opt-in from the start.
