Cursor reached a score of 90 on April 12, up from 89, extending a steady upward trend while GitHub Copilot slipped to 86 — its lowest point in our tracking period. The gap between the two has narrowed to just four points, a margin that would have seemed implausible three months ago.

## Anthropic: Advisor API Delivers Better Results at Lower Cost

Anthropic introduced a new Messages API feature called the Advisor strategy, which pairs Opus 4.6 as an advisor to cheaper models like Sonnet and Haiku. The approach yielded a 2.7 percentage point improvement on SWE-bench Multilingual while cutting per-task costs by 11.9%. That combination — higher accuracy and lower spend — is the kind of result that tends to shift enterprise procurement conversations.

The Advisor pattern works by having Opus 4.6 review and refine outputs from smaller models rather than handling entire tasks end-to-end. It's a tiered inference approach, and the benchmark numbers suggest it works.

## Anthropic: Mythos Preview Details Emerge

Additional details surfaced this week around the Claude Mythos Preview launched via Project Glasswing on April 7. The model has reportedly discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities during its limited deployment with 12 launch partners. Anthropic is providing $100 million in credits to open-source security organizations as part of the rollout — a substantial commitment that underscores how seriously the company is treating this as a security-first initiative, not just a model launch.

## OpenAI: GPT-5.3 Instant Mini Replaces GPT-5 Instant Mini

OpenAI quietly swapped out GPT-5 Instant Mini as ChatGPT's fallback model, replacing it with GPT-5.3 Instant Mini. The new model is described as delivering more natural conversation. The change is incremental but signals that OpenAI is actively managing the quality floor of its most widely used product.
