Two major Claude stories broke on March 28 and they point in the same direction: Anthropic is winning on both cultural and legal fronts simultaneously. Claude's app passed ChatGPT on the App Store free download chart — the first time any AI assistant has topped ChatGPT on that metric. Hours earlier, a federal judge blocked the Department of Defense's attempt to designate Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to allow Claude's use in autonomous weapons systems. Together, the two headlines represent a significant moment for Anthropic's public positioning.

## Claude Code: Tied with GitHub Copilot at 89

Claude Code reached 89 today — tying GitHub Copilot for the second spot behind ChatGPT. The climb from 83 on March 18 to 89 today, in just eleven days, is the fastest sustained rise this tool has seen in this tracking period. Claude AI also gained a point to 85, likely buoyed by the App Store news.

## Anthropic vs. the Pentagon

A federal judge blocked the DOD's effort to designate Anthropic a "supply chain risk." The designation was sought after Anthropic refused to make Claude available for autonomous weapons applications. The ruling is a significant legal moment: it establishes that a company can decline military use of its AI without being treated as a national security liability. Anthropic's position — that certain applications of Claude are incompatible with its safety principles — survived judicial scrutiny.

## Claude Tops the App Store

Claude passed ChatGPT on the App Store free chart for the first time. The timing is notable: it coincides with both the Pentagon ruling and the ongoing #QuitGPT movement (which gained momentum after the DOD-Anthropic dispute). Whether the App Store surge is durable or a news-cycle spike is the key question, but the symbolism is real.

## Gemini: Deep Think Goes Live

Gemini 3 Deep Think is now available for Gemini Ultra subscribers, with early API access also open. Deep Think is Google's answer to extended reasoning — a mode that spends more compute time deliberating before responding. The API access signals Google is ready to let developers build on the capability.

## Claude Mythos: Coverage Continues

Reporting on the leaked "Capybara" tier — the model above Opus described as a "step change" in capabilities — continued through the weekend. No release date has been announced. The story remains alive across developer communities.
