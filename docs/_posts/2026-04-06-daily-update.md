The Model Context Protocol reached a milestone that signals how quickly AI agent infrastructure is maturing: 97 million monthly SDK downloads, celebrated at MCP's first Dev Summit in New York. The week also saw Cursor consolidate its agent-first positioning, OpenAI extend the GPT-5.4 family down-market, and Anthropic confirm testing on a 10-trillion-parameter model.

## MCP Dev Summit: Infrastructure Moment

The MCP Dev Summit (April 2-3, NYC) drew 95+ sessions and confirmed that the protocol has become genuine infrastructure for AI agent development. The standout announcement was **x402** — a payment standard for AI agent transactions built on the MCP protocol. As agents increasingly take actions with real-world consequences (booking, purchasing, executing contracts), a standardized payment layer becomes critical. The x402 spec is still early, but the direction is clear.

## Cursor: Agent-First Platform Consolidated

Cursor 3 is now fully deployed, and the feature set is more cohesive than the initial launch notes suggested. **Composer 2** is now the default model, and **Cloud Handoff** enables seamless session transfer between local and cloud execution. The multi-repo agent capability — running agents across different repositories simultaneously — is the feature getting the most traction in developer communities. Cursor hit 90 in today's tracking.

## OpenAI: GPT-5.4 Mini and Nano

**GPT-5.4 mini** and **GPT-5.4 nano** complete the GPT-5.4 family, bringing flagship-class capability to high-volume workloads at lower cost. The nano model in particular targets applications where volume matters more than reasoning depth. **GPT-5.3 Codex Spark** remains in research preview at 1,000+ tokens/second.

## Google: Gemini 3 Flash as Default

Gemini 3 Flash became the default model in the Gemini app — a meaningful shift given that Flash outperforms Gemini 2.5 Pro at 3x speed. Google also released Gemma 4 open models, with the 31B variant ranking third on the Arena AI open model leaderboard.

## Anthropic: Claude Mythos Confirmed

Anthropic confirmed it is testing **Claude Mythos**, a 10-trillion-parameter model with enhanced reasoning, coding, and cybersecurity capabilities. The confirmation came via internal testing data that became public on April 2. At 10T parameters, Mythos would be roughly 5x larger than current frontier models. No release timeline was disclosed.

## Midjourney V8: Complete Engine Rewrite

Midjourney V8 launched with a completely rewritten inference engine, delivering 5x speed improvement and native 2K resolution output. For context: Kling 3.0 now supports up to 2-minute video clips — 5x longer than Sora's maximum — a benchmark that becomes more relevant as Sora approaches its April 26 app shutdown.

## Antigravity: Steepest Drop Yet

Antigravity fell three points to 52, its steepest single-day decline in our tracking period. With no substantive product news and the Cognition acquisition uncertainty unresolved, the tool is losing mindshare rapidly.
