Claude Code has broken out of its tie with Cursor. The tool climbed to 88 today — one point ahead of Cursor at 87 — riding momentum from Anthropic's 74 product releases in 52 days (February 3 through March 24). The separation is narrow but meaningful: Claude Code has now surpassed Cursor for the first time in this tracking period, driven by consistent shipping velocity rather than any single breakthrough feature.

## Claude Code: 74 Releases in 52 Days

The number tells the story. Anthropic has averaged more than one product release per day since early February, and the cumulative effect is showing in developer adoption. Claude Code's ARR crossed $2.5 billion — a revenue milestone that validates the pace of investment. For developers watching both tools, the contrast with Cursor's current post-Kimi-K2.5 reputational uncertainty has likely accelerated the divergence.

## Google Gemini: Flash Live Reaches 200+ Countries

Google announced Gemini 3.1 Flash Live on March 26 — a voice-first multimodal model that powers an expansion of Search Live to more than 200 countries. The model brings improvements to noise handling, tool use, and conversational quality specifically designed for real-time voice interactions. Flash Live represents Google's clearest statement yet that voice is a first-class interface for AI, not an afterthought.

## OpenAI: DeepSeek V4 Delay Confirmed

DeepSeek V4, originally expected in early March, has been pushed back to April 2026. The delay removes one anticipated pressure point from the competitive landscape, at least temporarily. OpenAI's own model roadmap benefits from the extra breathing room.

## Windsurf: Pricing Backlash Continues

The March 19 switch from credits to daily and weekly quotas continues to generate community friction. Developers who had built workflows around the credits model are adjusting, and some are vocal about the change's impact on their usage patterns. Windsurf's score holds at 75 — unchanged, but watched.
